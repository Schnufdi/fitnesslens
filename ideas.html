A full strategic review of MacroLens against the brief: genuinely novel insight engine vs AI summarisation tool. Scored, compared, and followed by specific fixes. Read this before pitching to anyone.

1 — Novelty assessment
Genuinely novel. But the novelty is invisible on arrival.
MacroLens is not an AI summarisation tool. The structural differentiator is real and significant: a 30-field schema that forces every story through causal chain analysis, bias checkpoints, second-order country impacts, framework attribution, counterintuitive questions, and resolution scenarios. No other daily publication has this architecture. ChatGPT can produce one of these fields in 30 seconds. It cannot produce all 30 consistently, at the quality level of someone with 18 years of institutional finance experience, every day.

The problem is that the novelty is in the depth — and depth is invisible until a reader has been reading for 10 minutes. The first 30 seconds of the MacroLens experience does not yet communicate "this is structurally different from anything else you read." The entry point is better since the redesign. But the positioning — "Economic theory, applied daily" — tells you the method, not the benefit. It says what the product is, not what it does for you.
2 — Gap analysis
The product is stronger than the experience of it.
Does it feel like summaries? Sometimes yes. The 90-second version in the entry point reads like a good summary. The impact cards feel like highlights. The mechanism section and causal chain do not feel like summaries — they feel like analysis. The problem is sequencing: the summary-feeling parts come first and set a mental model that undersells what follows.

Is second-order thinking strong enough? The causal chain is excellent — 5 steps, each with a theory attribution and a mechanism explanation. The ripple effects and global picture show third and fourth-order consequences. The bias checkpoints explicitly challenge the analysis. This is strong. The weakness is that these sections are labelled with passive section titles ("Ripple effects", "The global picture") rather than provocative ones that signal this is unusual analysis.

Does it deliver an edge? On the Tencent story: yes. "The Western financial press covers Tencent through a geopolitical lens. The actual business story is underweighted." That is an edge — a specific reframe of what everyone else is covering. But it is in the "What consensus misses" subsection, four scrolls deep. Most readers will never reach it. The product buries its sharpest insights.
3 — Output quality review
The insights are not obvious. But they are not consistently sharp either.
Could a user replicate this in ChatGPT in 30 seconds? No. Not the causal chain with theory attribution. Not the bias checkpoints. Not the resolution scenarios with probability weightings. Not the structured country impact analysis. The product has genuine analytical architecture that requires both the schema and 18 years of institutional knowledge to produce consistently.

Where the insights are sharp: The pullquotes ("Tencent did not build AI. It applied AI to what it already had.") The counterintuitive questions (III — "If AI is doing all the work, what does Tencent's human workforce actually do?" — is a genuinely provocative question). The "Said vs meant" section. The bias checkpoint on Western analytical framework applied to a Chinese platform.

Where the insights are generic: The "So what for me" investor points read like Bloomberg commentary. "Tencent at current valuations prices in significant regulatory discount" is something any analyst would say. The signal analysis ("Tencent Gross Margin ↑ 53%") is factual but the insight density in the explanation is variable. Some signal explanations are sharp. Others are descriptive rather than analytical.

The counterintuitive questions are the product's highest-value output. They are currently the most buried. This is backwards.

4 — Specific product improvements

Move one counterintuitive to the entry point — between the pullquote and the 90-second summary.
Build time: 30 minutes. Impact on conversion: high. The counterintuitive is the hook. Use it first.
Highest impact

"Ripple effects" says nothing. "Who else gets hurt that nobody is talking about" says everything. "The global picture" is a geography lesson. "The countries that win and lose — and why the obvious answer is wrong" creates a reason to read. Every section title is currently a label. Every section title should be a provocation. The sections are excellent — they are just introduced as if they are filing cabinet drawers rather than intellectual challenges.
Suggested renames: "Market signals" → "What markets already knew". "The mechanism" → "The thing driving this that the headlines missed". "Resolution scenario" → "How this ends — three versions".
High impact
Add a "What everyone else got wrong" field
The "consensus missing" field in the readBetweenLines section ("The Western financial press covers Tencent through a geopolitical lens — the actual business story is underweighted") is the sharpest, most differentiated content in any story. It exists in the JSON schema. It is displayed inside a collapsed tab four scrolls down. It needs to be a first-class, prominently displayed section — not a sub-tab in "Reading between the lines". This single field is worth the subscription.

High impact
The causal chain needs a "therefore what?" closing beat
The causal chain ends at Step 5 ("Capital efficiency improves: capex falls while returns rise") with an explanation. It then stops. The most powerful thing a causal chain can do is show the reader the implication they had not reached on their own. Add a final "therefore what?" beat: "Therefore: the Western analyst assuming AI requires a capital-intensive buildout phase is working from the wrong model. Tencent is the evidence." That is the edge. The chain currently demonstrates the mechanism. It should also deliver the verdict.
Add a "Chain verdict" field to the JSON schema. Render it as a highlighted closing statement below the final step. One sentence, sharp, specific to the story.

Consider collapsing to one voice — the serious finance professional — and making every point genuinely actionable.

Medium impact

The AI ecosystem map shows which layers are active for this story (Monetisation layer lit up for Tencent). It looks good. It should show something non-obvious: "This story appears to be about monetisation but the structural driver is at the Infrastructure layer — which is why the Western narrative about AI spend is misdirected." The map should be a thesis, not an illustration.
Add a "map insight" field to the ecosystemMap schema: one sentence explaining why the active layers are analytically surprising or significant. Display it prominently inside the map section.
