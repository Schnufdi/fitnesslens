The protocol cards in Today expand into the science when you tap them — this page collects it all in one place and lets you filter by category. The myths are the ones most worth reading first. Science briefing
↯ Myth
More calories accelerates muscle gain in advanced lifters
An advanced lifter gains 0.5–1kg lean mass per month under ideal conditions. This is a physiological ceiling — not a mindset limitation. A surplus above +300 kcal adds fat on top of lean mass without accelerating muscle protein synthesis. The +200 kcal is calibrated to the actual rate of accretion.
↯ Myth
Knee injury means no leg training
The most counterproductive belief in MSK rehab. Complete rest weakens surrounding muscles, reduces joint stability, and deconditions support structures. Appropriate loading is the primary stimulus for connective tissue adaptation. The research consistently shows controlled progressive loading improves functional outcomes for degenerative tears more reliably than rest.
↯ Myth
Carbs at night cause fat gain
Total caloric balance over 24–48 hours determines fat accumulation — not meal timing. Insulin sensitivity is elevated post-training, making the evening post-workout window one of the best times to consume carbohydrates. The dinner carbs in this programme are mechanistically correct, not a concession.
↯ Myth
High reps tone, low reps bulk
Both rep ranges build muscle when taken close to failure. "Toning" is fat loss revealing muscle — it is not a distinct training adaptation. This programme uses 6–20 rep ranges to maximise mechanical tension at lower reps and metabolic stress at higher reps simultaneously, not for aesthetic classification.
↯ Myth
The post-workout window is 30 minutes — always
The urgency of post-workout protein applies when training fasted. If you ate a protein-containing meal 60–90 min before training, amino acids are still elevated at session end. What matters: hit total protein across the day. The 30-minute window is relevant for morning fasted training — which is your actual situation, so the advice stands, but for the right reason.
⟳ Synergy
Posterior chain training produces systemic anabolism
Heavy hip hinge and glute work produces the largest acute hormonal response of any training category — testosterone and GH elevation proportional to muscle mass recruited and total load. Friday's session creates an anabolic environment that accelerates recovery across all muscle groups, not just the posterior chain trained that day.
⟳ Synergy
Sleep and testosterone: one week of disruption causes measurable damage
Leproult & Van Cauter (2011): restricting sleep to 5 hours for one week reduced testosterone by 10–15% — equivalent to ageing 10–15 years. The same window drives GH and IGF-1 release for joint repair. Poor sleep simultaneously suppresses muscle gain and slows meniscus recovery.
⟳ Synergy
Leucine threshold: MPS is triggered by one amino acid, not total protein
mTOR activation requires ~2–3g leucine per meal as a threshold event. 185g across four meals (~46g per meal) consistently clears the threshold at every window. 185g across two meals leaves two MPS activation windows empty. Protein distribution is pharmacokinetics, not preference.
⟳ Synergy
Twice-weekly frequency produces ~70% more hypertrophy at matched volume
Schoenfeld et al. (2016) meta-analysis: training each muscle group twice weekly produces significantly more hypertrophy than once-weekly at the same total volume. MPS elevation from a session decays within 48–72 hours — frequency timing re-stimulates before MPS returns to baseline.
◈ Fact
Each kg of lean mass raises resting metabolism by ~13 kcal — permanently
Adding 3kg lean mass raises RMR by ~40 kcal/day permanently. At 44, this directly counteracts the 1–2% per decade metabolic rate decline. The lean mass built in this mesocycle compounds metabolically for decades. Body composition matters more than body weight as a long-term health marker.
◈ Fact
90 minutes post-wake is the optimal morning training window
Core body temperature, testosterone, and neuromuscular activation all reach their morning peak approximately 90 minutes after waking. Training immediately on waking means training at biological low tide. The 90-minute activation window — hydration, food, supplements — is preparation, not delay.
◈ Fact
The eccentric phase is where hypertrophy stimulus is greatest
Mechanical tension during the lengthening phase — the controlled descent — creates more muscle damage and metabolic stress than the concentric contraction. A 2–3 second eccentric on every rep is the training mechanism, not a technique cue. Dropping the weight quickly discards the majority of each rep's hypertrophic value.
⚠ Warning
Swelling within 24h means load was excessive — do not train through it
Post-exercise knee swelling is an intra-articular inflammatory response. Arthrogenic muscle inhibition — fluid in the joint mechanically suppresses activation of surrounding musculature — makes training through swelling simultaneously harmful and ineffective. Reduce depth and load 30% for the following session. Do not progress depth until two consecutive clean sessions.
⚠ Warning
Chronic stress creates a biochemical environment where training cannot adapt
Cortisol directly suppresses testosterone and inhibits MPS. High sustained stress makes training nearly useless at the cellular level — the stimulus lands but the adaptation signal is blocked. Sleep, rest days, and stress management are the physiological conditions required for the programme to function. They are not lifestyle recommendations.
⚠ Warning
Alcohol post-training directly inhibits mTOR
Ethanol suppresses mTOR signalling independently of calories or sleep disruption. 1–2 drinks post-training = ~15% MPS suppression. 3–4 drinks = ~25%. 5+ = near-complete cancellation of the session's anabolic signal. The mechanism is direct, not indirect. If drinking on training days, hit the post-workout protein window first.
◈ Knee
Strong glutes reduce medial compartment load — you are training the knee's defenders
The medial meniscus absorbs 50–70% of medial compartment compressive load. Glute maximus and medius strength directly control femoral alignment and valgus collapse — the primary overload mechanism for medial meniscal tissue. Every hip thrust and RDL in this programme is structural knee medicine with muscle gain as a side effect.
◈ Knee
Degenerative tears are not fragile — appropriate loading is the intervention
A degenerative meniscal tear is gradual fibrocartilage breakdown over years — not a clean acute rupture. Appropriate loading does not worsen the tear. Extended rest weakens surrounding structures and reduces joint stability. Controlled progressive loading improves functional outcomes for degenerative tears more reliably than conservative rest — the evidence is consistent.
◈ Knee
Articular cartilage has no blood supply — it feeds through movement
Cartilage receives nutrients exclusively via synovial fluid, which only circulates during joint movement. Rest day cycling and walking are not optional cardio — they are cartilage nutrition delivery. Prolonged sitting with the knee bent for hours is more damaging to cartilage than controlled movement under appropriate load.
◈ Knee
Collagen + Vitamin C doubles connective tissue synthesis — timing is the variable
Shaw et al. (2019): 15g collagen peptides + 50mg vitamin C taken 45–60 minutes before exercise doubled collagen synthesis in tendons and ligaments vs. placebo. Vitamin C is required for hydroxylation of proline in collagen. Exercise increases collagen turnover — the supplement primes the pool at exactly the right moment. This is the most targeted meniscus intervention available without a prescription.
● Supplement
Creatine monohydrate: the only supplement with tier-1 evidence across all training populations
Hundreds of RCTs. Consistent 5–15% strength and power output improvement via expanded phosphocreatine stores. No cycling needed. No loading required — 5g daily works identically to a loading protocol after day 28. Monohydrate is identical to more expensive forms. If you are not on creatine, you are leaving the most accessible performance gain untaken.
● Supplement
Magnesium glycinate: sleep quality and 300 enzymatic reactions including MPS
Most people are deficient. Glycinate form is superior to magnesium oxide for absorption and avoids GI side effects. Relevant pathways: NMDA receptor regulation (sleep depth), ATP synthesis (energy production), and direct involvement in mRNA translation during muscle protein synthesis. Before-bed timing targets sleep architecture first.
● Supplement
Ashwagandha KSM-66: cortisol reduction with genuine testosterone RCT data
Multiple RCTs in resistance-trained men: 10–15% testosterone increase, significant cortisol reduction vs. placebo. KSM-66 standardisation matters — generic ashwagandha shows inconsistent results. At 44 with high training stress, the testosterone-to-cortisol ratio is a meaningful lever. Additive benefit on sleep quality when taken consistently.
● Supplement
Vitamin D3 deficiency accelerates cartilage degradation — not just bone
UK latitude means most people are deficient October–April. D3 is directly involved in chondrocyte function and type II collagen synthesis in articular cartilage — the tissue your MRI flagged with chondromalacia. D3+K2 combination: K2 ensures calcium is directed to bone, not arteries. 3000–4000 IU daily with a fat-containing meal for absorption.
● Supplement
Omega-3: EPA and DHA compete with arachidonic acid at the COX enzyme level
EPA and DHA don't just "reduce inflammation" generally. They compete directly with arachidonic acid for COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes — reducing prostaglandin E2 synthesis specifically. PGE2 is the primary inflammatory mediator in synovial tissue. This is both a performance supplement and targeted joint medicine. Non-negotiable given the meniscus condition.
⧗ Peri-workout
Sauna post-training: net anabolic if executed correctly
Post-workout sauna activates heat shock proteins that enhance muscle protein folding and repair. Protocol: protein first, then sauna under 20 minutes, 500ml water before entering, electrolytes after. Beyond 30 minutes post-training, cortisol elevation starts competing with MPS and the benefit is cancelled. Sequence is non-negotiable: protein → sauna.
⧗ Peri-workout
Carbs pre-training protect muscle — not just performance
When glycogen depletes mid-session, the body catabolises muscle protein to maintain blood glucose. This is not a performance issue — it is net-negative for the session's anabolic outcome. Pre-workout carbs are muscle protection. The high-GI dates and banana before Friday's session are not comfort food; they are structural.
⧗ Peri-workout
The pre-sleep meal is a training intervention, not a late snack
GH is released during slow-wave sleep, 60–90 minutes after sleep onset. The amino acids available when GH peaks determine how much protein synthesis occurs overnight. Cottage cheese before bed provides casein protein that digests over 6–8 hours — ensuring amino acid availability aligns with the GH pulse. This is intentional engineering, not habit.
⧗ Peri-workout
Thursday's carb load fuels Friday's session — not Friday's breakfast
Muscle glycogen replenishment takes 12–24 hours from carbohydrate ingestion. Friday morning's oats are insufficient to fully stock glycogen stores by the time the RDL starts. Thursday afternoon's carbs are the primary fuel for Friday's session. This is why the pre-posterior-chain rest day protocol starts the carb load at Thursday lunch.
