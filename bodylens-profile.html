The three laws — override everything
I
Protein is non-negotiable.
Every day. No exceptions.
185–190g daily across four meals. Each meal must clear the leucine threshold to trigger mTOR activation — the molecular switch for muscle protein synthesis. Total protein matters. Distribution matters equally. A missed meal doesn't average out; it leaves an MPS window empty and irretrievable.
Why law, not guideline
At 44, MPS efficiency declines. The gap between adequate and inadequate protein is larger than at 25. Undereating protein on rest days is the single most common reason advanced lifters stall. MPS peaks on rest days — the amino acid supply matters precisely when you feel least inclined to eat purposefully.
II
The posterior chain protects
the knee. Training it is medicine.
Strong glutes and hamstrings reduce medial compartment load by 30–40%, directly reducing stress on the torn meniscal tissue. Every RDL and hip thrust is structural joint therapy with a side effect of muscle gain. The two goals are not in conflict — they are the same goal.
Why law, not guideline
The instinct after a knee injury is to reduce leg training. This is the single most counterproductive response possible. The posterior chain is the knee's external support system. Reducing it accelerates degradation. Friday is posterior chain because it is the therapeutic priority.
III
Sleep is training.
Skipping it skips the adaptation.
GH release peaks during slow-wave sleep 60–90 minutes after onset. Testosterone consolidates overnight. Connective tissue remodelling runs on the same hormonal cascade. One week of sub-6h sleep reduces testosterone by 10–15%. The gym session is the stimulus. Sleep is where the body responds.
Why law, not guideline
Most people treat sleep as the variable they sacrifice. From a physiology standpoint, this is equivalent to training and then not eating. The stimulus was delivered; the response was blocked. 8 hours is not a preference — it is the minimum operating condition.
Supporting principles
T
Training
5 governing principles
T1
Progressive overload is the only mechanism
Add one set or a small amount of load each week on at least one compound movement. Showing up without progression is maintenance. The number must move.
T2
Twice-weekly frequency outperforms volume concentration
MPS from a session decays within 48–72h. The push/pull/posterior/upper split ensures every major muscle group is stimulated twice weekly — the evidence-based frequency for maximising hypertrophy at matched volume.
T3
The deload is supercompensation, not rest
Accumulated fatigue masks fitness gains. Week 4 reduces volume 40% while maintaining load. Fatigue clears; fitness rebounds above the pre-fatigue baseline. Skipping the deload means never collecting the dividend.
T4
The eccentric is the stimulus, not the lift
Mechanical tension during the lengthening phase drives hypertrophy more than the concentric. A 2–3 second controlled descent is the training mechanism — not a technique cue.
T5
RPE governs load — not ego, not spreadsheets
RPE 8 means two reps left in the tank. The number on the bar is feedback. The effort is the prescription.
N
Nutrition
5 governing principles
N1
Carbs are training fuel — not the enemy
Training in a glycogen-depleted state catabolises muscle protein. Carbs are front-loaded on training days and timed around sessions. Reducing on rest days is appropriate. Eliminating is not.
N2
The surplus is small because the physiology is slow
Advanced lifters gain 0.5–1kg lean mass per month — a physiological ceiling. A surplus above +300 kcal adds fat without accelerating gains. The +200 kcal is calibrated to actual accretion rate.
N3
Fat is a hormonal floor — never drop below it
Testosterone synthesis requires dietary fat. Below ~0.7g/kg, testosterone drops measurably. Omega-3s are membrane components in synovial tissue. Fat is structural, not caloric noise.
N4
Functional food over recipes — simplicity sustains
The weekly kit is 11 ingredients. Every meal: protein + starch + vegetable + fat. Complexity destroys consistency. The best plan is the one you actually execute.
N5
Anti-inflammatory eating is part of the programme
Omega-3s, turmeric, olive oil, and dark leafy greens are specific interventions for the synovial environment. This is not optional for your profile.
