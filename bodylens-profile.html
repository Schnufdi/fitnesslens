The three laws — these override everything else
I
Protein is non-negotiable. Every day. No exceptions.
185–190g daily across four meals. Each meal must clear the leucine threshold (~2–3g) to trigger mTOR activation — the molecular switch for muscle protein synthesis. Total protein matters. Distribution matters equally. A missed meal doesn't average out; it leaves an MPS window empty and irretrievable.
Why this is law, not guideline
At 44, muscle protein synthesis efficiency declines. The gap between adequate and inadequate protein is larger than at 25. Undereating protein on rest days is the single most common reason advanced lifters stall. Rest days are when MPS from the previous session peaks — the amino acid supply matters most precisely when you feel least inclined to eat purposefully.
II
The posterior chain protects the knee. Training it is medicine.
Strong glutes and hamstrings reduce medial compartment compressive load by 30–40%, directly reducing stress on the torn meniscal tissue. Glute maximus controls femoral alignment. Hamstrings resist anterior tibial translation. Every RDL and hip thrust is structural joint therapy with a side effect of muscle gain.
Why this is law, not guideline
The instinct after a knee injury is to reduce leg training. This is the single most counterproductive response. The posterior chain is the knee's external support system. Reducing it accelerates degradation. Friday is posterior chain precisely because it is the therapeutic priority — not because it is convenient.
III
Sleep is training. Skipping it skips the adaptation.
GH release peaks during slow-wave sleep 60–90 minutes after sleep onset. Testosterone consolidates overnight. Connective tissue remodelling — including the meniscus — runs on the same hormonal cascade. One week of sub-6h sleep reduces testosterone by 10–15%. The gym session is the stimulus. Sleep is where the body responds to it.
Why this is law, not guideline
Most people treat sleep as the variable they sacrifice when life is busy. From a physiology standpoint, this is equivalent to doing the training and then not eating. The stimulus was delivered; the response was blocked. 8 hours is not a preference — it is the minimum operating condition for this programme to function.
Supporting principles — training and nutrition
Training principles
T1
Progressive overload is the only mechanism
Add one set or a small amount of load each week on at least one compound movement. Showing up consistently without progression is maintenance, not improvement. The number must move.
T2
Twice-weekly frequency outperforms volume concentration
MPS from a session decays within 48–72h. The push/pull/posterior/upper split ensures every major muscle group is stimulated twice weekly — the evidence-based frequency for maximising hypertrophy at matched volume.
T3
The deload is supercompensation, not rest
Accumulated fatigue masks fitness gains. Week 4 reduces volume 40% while maintaining load. Fatigue clears; fitness rebounds above the pre-fatigue baseline. Skipping the deload means never collecting the dividend.
T4
The eccentric is the stimulus, not the lift
Mechanical tension during the lengthening phase drives hypertrophy more than the concentric. A 2–3 second controlled descent is not caution — it is the training mechanism. Dropping the weight rapidly discards most of each rep's value.
T5
RPE governs load — not ego, not spreadsheets
RPE 8 means two reps left in the tank. If a weight gets you to RPE 6, add load. If it takes you to 9.5, reduce. The number on the bar is feedback. The effort is the prescription.
Nutrition principles
N1
Carbs are training fuel — not the enemy
Training in a glycogen-depleted state catabolises muscle protein to maintain blood glucose — the opposite of the goal. Carbs are front-loaded on training days and timed around sessions. Reducing them on rest days is appropriate. Eliminating them is not.
N2
The surplus is small because the physiology is slow
An advanced lifter gains 0.5–1kg lean mass per month — a physiological ceiling, not a mindset limitation. A surplus above +300 kcal adds fat without accelerating muscle gain. The +200 kcal is calibrated to actual accretion rate.
N3
Fat is a hormonal floor — never drop below it
Testosterone synthesis requires dietary fat. Below ~0.7g/kg, testosterone drops measurably. Omega-3s are membrane components in synovial tissue. D3 absorption requires fat. Fat is structural, not caloric noise.
N4
Functional food over recipes — simplicity sustains
The weekly kit is 11 ingredients. Every meal: protein source + starch + vegetable + fat. Complexity destroys consistency. 500g chicken + rice + olive oil executed daily beats an elaborate plan followed twice a week.
N5
Anti-inflammatory eating is built into the programme
Omega-3s, turmeric, olive oil, and dark leafy greens are not lifestyle choices — they are specific interventions for the synovial environment. Seed oils, excess fructose, and processed meats work against the knee's recovery. This is not optional for your profile.

Supplement stack — what works and why
Tier 1 — Non-negotiable
Tier-1 evidence. Directly relevant to your goals and constraints.
Creatine monohydrate
5g daily · any time
Hundreds of RCTs. Consistent 5–15% strength output improvement via phosphocreatine expansion. No loading, no cycling, no expensive alternatives needed.
Collagen + Vitamin C
15g + 50mg · 45–60 min pre-training
Shaw et al. (2019): doubles connective tissue collagen synthesis. Vitamin C is required for hydroxylation of proline in collagen. The most targeted meniscus intervention available.
Omega-3 EPA + DHA
2–3g combined · with fat meal
Competes with arachidonic acid for COX enzymes — reduces prostaglandin E2, the primary inflammatory mediator in synovial tissue. Both performance and joint medicine.
Vitamin D3 + K2
3000 IU + 100mcg · with fat meal
UK latitude = near-universal deficiency. D3 drives testosterone synthesis and chondrocyte maintenance. K2 directs calcium to bone. Non-negotiable October–April.
Magnesium glycinate
400mg · before bed
Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions including MPS. Glycinate absorbs without GI issues. Primary function: deepens slow-wave sleep, amplifying the GH release window.
Ashwagandha KSM-66
600mg · any time
Multiple RCTs: 10–15% testosterone increase, significant cortisol reduction. KSM-66 standardisation is critical — generic ashwagandha inconsistent. Additive sleep quality benefit.
