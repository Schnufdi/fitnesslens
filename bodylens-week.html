From your coach
This is how the week is structured — and why it is structured this way. Push on Monday so chest and shoulders have two full days of recovery before the second upper hit on Saturday. Pull on Wednesday so the back recovers before Saturday's row. Posterior chain on Friday because it is the most demanding session and benefits from Thursday's full rest, a pre-loaded glycogen store, and the largest appetite for anabolism at the end of the hard-training window. The calorie variance across the week is deliberate: training days carry the full surplus, rest days sit below it. The week is one integrated system. No session exists in isolation.

Programme architecture
Weeks 1–2 · Now
Accumulation
8–12 rep range. Building volume to 14–16 sets/muscle/week. Each session adds one working set to one compound movement vs the previous week. Fatigue accumulates deliberately.
Week 3
Intensification
Rep range drops to 5–8. Load increases. Volume holds. The accumulated neuromuscular adaptation from weeks 1–2 is expressed as higher strength output. This is when you feel the weeks 1–2 investment pay.
Week 4
Deload — supercompensation
–40% volume, same load. Accumulated fatigue clears. Fitness rebounds above the pre-fatigue baseline. This is the dividend that weeks 1–3 are building toward. Do not skip it.

Weekly modifications — knee protocol
Friday only — 3 modifications
Squat → RDL + Hip Thrust. Hip hinge replaces knee-dominant movement. Net stimulus ~95%.
Lunge → Step-up (20cm). Controlled unilateral. Full quad-glute chain. Net stimulus 100%.
Full leg press → Partial 0–70°. +20–30% load to offset shortened ROM. Net stimulus ~90%.
Wednesday — 1 modification
Bent-over row → Chest-supported row. Removes standing hip hinge isometric hold. Primary mid-back stimulus identical. Net stimulus 100%.
