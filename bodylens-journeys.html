Avoid this week — and why
Not prohibition — optimisation. Each of these is working against specific goals right now.
✕
Refined seed oils (sunflower, vegetable, corn)
High omega-6 linoleic acid competes with omega-3 for COX enzymes — directly amplifying the inflammatory pathway you're suppressing for the knee. Use olive oil or butter only.
✕
Alcohol post-training
Ethanol inhibits mTOR signalling directly — the exact pathway leucine activates. Even 2–3 drinks post-session suppresses MPS by ~25% and disrupts overnight GH release. Drink on full rest days if at all, never training days.
✕
High-fructose foods on rest days
Fructose is metabolised in the liver, not stored as muscle glycogen. On rest days when glycogen demand is low, excess fructose converts to liver fat. Fine pre-training; poor choice when inactive.
✕
Processed deli meats as protein sources
High sodium disrupts fluid balance. Nitrites in processed meats are associated with low-grade systemic inflammation. Use whole cuts — better protein quality, no additives.
✕
Large meals within 90 min of training
Fat and fibre slow gastric emptying significantly. A heavy meal close to compound work diverts blood to digestion, raises RPE, and can cause nausea under load. The pre-workout meal is specifically designed to be fast-digesting.
✕
Protein bars as primary protein sources
Most use hydrolysed collagen or low-quality blends that don't clear the leucine threshold for MPS activation. Chicken, eggs, Greek yoghurt, and fish have superior amino acid profiles for muscle synthesis.
