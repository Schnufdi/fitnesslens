BodyLens · Business strategy
From personal tool to performance product
BodyLens is currently a personalised single-user performance OS. The path to a business runs through deepening the personalisation engine, adding the data layer that makes it sticky, and gating the most valuable features behind a subscription that is priced well below a personal trainer or nutritionist.
Market positioning
The insight
Every performance app currently on the market solves one layer. MyFitnessPal tracks food. Strava tracks cardio. Strong tracks lifts. Nike Training gives you programmes. No product integrates all four layers into a single coherent system that knows who you are and adapts to your specific constraints.
BodyLens is not a fitness app. It is a performance operating system — the first to connect training stimulus, nutrition timing, sleep, supplementation, and injury management into one adaptive daily system. The AI coach makes it feel like having a £200/hour professional in your pocket.
The moat
🔒
Depth of personalisation
Profile includes injury MRI findings, training age, nutrition journey, wake time, and 12-week arc. No generic app can replicate this from day one. The profile IS the product.
🧠
AI coach with context
The system prompt carries the full user profile. Every AI answer is anchored to the person's specific goals, constraints, and history. Generic AI assistants cannot compete with this specificity.
📈
Longitudinal data
Session check-ins, supplement adherence, knee status, and protein tracking build a dataset over 12 weeks that no competitor has for that user. The product gets more valuable with use.
🎯
Constraint integration
The meniscus protocol is the proof of concept: a specific medical constraint that reconfigures every movement, every food choice, and every supplement recommendation. This is the differentiator.

Go-to-market strategy
Phase 1 — Personal proof (now)
Run the programme on yourself for 12 weeks. Document every milestone, every adaptation, every protocol decision. The founder using the product is the strongest marketing signal in the consumer health space. The story of a senior professional returning to tennis after a knee event while building lean mass at 44 is commercially compelling and deeply human.
Phase 2 — Closed beta (months 2–3)
10–20 users, curated. Ideally: similar profile (35–50, former athletes returning to training, one specific constraint). Each user goes through onboarding, gets a personalised profile build, and uses the app for 8 weeks. Feedback loop informs v2. Community of early users is the product's first marketing channel.
Phase 3 — Controlled launch (month 4+)
Waitlist-driven public launch. Content strategy built around the science rail — short-form explanations of the mechanisms behind common fitness myths. SEO-optimised long-form content targeting "performance programme for 40s", "returning to training after knee injury", "lean muscle after 40". The science content builds trust and drives organic acquisition.
Channel strategy
Primary: content-led SEO. The science rail in the app becomes a blog. Every science card is a 600-word article. The myths section is a "common mistakes" series. Long-tail search traffic for performance and injury-return content converts to trial.
Secondary: physiotherapy and sports medicine referral. The meniscus protocol is a clinically coherent intervention. Physios and sports doctors who see compliant patients will recommend the tool. B2B2C referral channel.
Tertiary: LinkedIn and professional network. The founder profile — senior finance, endurance sport background, recovery story — maps to a large addressable audience of professionals in their 40s who have let performance slip and want a structured return.

BodyLens · Technical roadmap
From static HTML to adaptive performance system
Current stack: pure static HTML/CSS/JS, no build step, no backend, no database. Deployable to GitHub Pages in under 10 minutes. The roadmap moves from static personalisation toward a real-time adaptive system with data persistence, wearable integration, and multi-user support — without losing the simplicity that makes the current version feel fast and owned.

Development roadmap — ranked by impact / effort
Now · 1–2 days
LocalStorage persistence for check-ins and preferences
Session check-in data, wake time preference, selected nutrition journey, and supplement adherence all written to localStorage. Zero infrastructure, zero cost, transforms the app from stateless to memory-bearing.
Low effort · High impact
Now · 1 day
Progressive Web App (PWA) wrapper
Add manifest.json and service worker. Enables "Add to Home Screen" on iPhone and Android — app icon, offline support for cached pages, full-screen mode. No App Store, no Xcode, no £79/year developer account.
Low effort · High impact
Now · 2–3 days
API key proxy — Cloudflare Worker or Vercel Edge Function
Move the Anthropic API call server-side to a lightweight edge function. The API key leaves the client entirely. Required before sharing the app with any user other than yourself. Cloudflare Workers free tier handles ~100k requests/day — sufficient for beta.
Medium effort · Critical for scaling
Next · 1 week
Supabase backend — auth, profiles, and check-in storage
Supabase (open-source Firebase alternative) provides Postgres database, auth, and real-time APIs. User signs up, profile is stored, check-ins persist across devices. Free tier handles the first 500 users. Migration from localStorage to Supabase is a clean upgrade path.
Medium effort · Unlocks multi-user
Next · 3–5 days
Dynamic programme builder — replace hardcoded HTML data
Move all programme data (exercises, protocols, meals, modifications) to JSON files or a Supabase table. HTML becomes a template. Programme updates become data changes, not code changes. Prerequisite for supporting multiple users with different programmes.
Medium effort · Foundation for scale
Next · 1 week
Onboarding wizard — profile builder for new users
A 12-step onboarding flow that collects: age, weight, training history, goals, constraints (with injury checklist), nutrition journey preference, wake time, supplement baseline, and training days. Outputs a personalised system prompt and programme configuration. The AI fills in the gaps. This turns BodyLens from a single-user tool into a product.
High effort · Required for product
Later · 2–3 weeks
Wearable integration — Oura Ring, Whoop, Garmin
OAuth connection to wearable APIs. Nightly HRV (Heart Rate Variability), sleep score, and readiness score feed into the next day's protocol. Low readiness triggers an automatic RPE reduction and volume adjustment. The protocol becomes adaptive in real-time rather than prescribed.
High effort · Pro tier differentiator
Later · 1–2 weeks
AI programme generation — new mesocycle from check-in data
After the 4-week deload, the AI reviews 4 weeks of check-in data (compliance, knee status, protein adherence, strength feedback) and generates the week 5–8 intensification programme automatically. The protocol evolves based on actual performance, not assumed progression.
High effort · Core value prop for Pro
Later · 3–5 days
Blood biomarker integration — lab result upload
User uploads blood test PDF (NHS, Medichecks, Thriva). OCR extracts testosterone, vitamin D, ferritin, HbA1c, and other relevant markers. The supplement recommendations and nutrition targets update automatically. A Vit D deficiency changes the D3 recommendation. A low testosterone triggers a review of the sleep and supplement protocol.
Medium effort · High clinical value
Later · 1 week
Capacitor native app — iOS and Android packaging
Wrap the web app in Capacitor to produce App Store and Play Store submissions. Access to native push notifications (supplement reminders, session alerts). Requires Apple Developer Account (£79/year) and Google Play (£20 one-time). Total build time approximately 8 hours once the web app is stable.
Medium effort · Distribution reach
